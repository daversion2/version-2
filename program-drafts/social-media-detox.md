# Social Media Detox — 30 Days

## Interview Notes

- **Philosophy:** Dopamine/attention, identity/comparison, and time reclamation — woven together
- **Scope:** All passive consumption — anything with an algorithmic feed or infinite scroll. Only direct messaging allowed.
- **Cold Turkey:** Delete all apps Day 1, zero social media for 30 days. Recommended mode.
- **Gradual Build:** Usage reduction — 30 min/day → 15 min → 5 min → zero
- **Daily format:** Consistent core challenge, rotating educational content (equal mix of manipulation/design and personal impact)
- **Difficulty curve (Cold Turkey):** 4 (days 1-7) → 3 (days 8-14) → 2 (days 15-30)
- **Badge:** "Scroll Breaker" — 25 points
- **Icon:** share-social-outline, color: #3F51B5
- **Category:** Mind
- **Suggested habits:** "20 minutes social media per day", "Social media only on desktop"

## Cold Turkey Mode

### Day 1
- **Challenge:** No social media — delete all apps
- **Description:** Delete every social media and algorithmic feed app from your phone — Instagram, TikTok, X, Facebook, Snapchat, Reddit, YouTube, and any others with an infinite scroll. This isn't hiding them in a folder. Delete them. You can reinstall in 30 days if you choose. Direct messaging apps (iMessage, WhatsApp, Signal) are fine. Today starts your 30-day detox.
- **Success Criteria:** All social media and algorithmic feed apps deleted. No social media consumed today.
- **Difficulty:** 4
- **Category:** Mind
- **Educational Title:** You're Not the Customer — You're the Product
- **Educational Content:** Social media companies don't sell you a product. They sell your attention to advertisers. Every feature — infinite scroll, autoplay, pull-to-refresh, notification badges — is engineered to maximize the time you spend on the app. Former Facebook VP Chamath Palihapitiya said these tools are "ripping apart the social fabric of how society works." You're not quitting an app. You're reclaiming your attention from a trillion-dollar industry designed to capture it.
- **Tip:** Before deleting, take screenshots of anything you genuinely need (saved posts, contacts). Then delete without hesitation.

### Day 2
- **Challenge:** No social media
- **Description:** Day 2 without social media. You'll notice phantom urges — reaching for your phone during idle moments, muscle memory pulling you toward where an app used to be. This is normal. The habit loop is firing, but there's nothing to reward it.
- **Success Criteria:** No social media consumed today.
- **Difficulty:** 4
- **Category:** Mind
- **Educational Title:** Phantom Scrolling — Your Brain on Autopilot
- **Educational Content:** Your brain has built an automatic routine around social media — boredom triggers the urge, your thumb moves to the app, and dopamine rewards the behavior. When you remove the app, the trigger and urge still fire. You'll catch yourself opening your phone with no purpose, or reaching for an app that isn't there. This is your habit loop running on autopilot. Each time you notice it without acting, the loop weakens.
- **Neuroscience Note:** Habitual phone checking bypasses conscious decision-making entirely. It's controlled by the basal ganglia, the same brain region that automates driving and tying shoes.
- **Tip:** When you catch yourself reaching for your phone, pause and ask: "What was I actually looking for?" The answer is usually "nothing."

- **Success Criteria:** No social media consumed today.
- **Difficulty:** 4
- **Category:** Mind
- **Educational Title:** Boredom Is the Point
- **Educational Content:** Social media has trained your brain to avoid boredom at all costs. Any micro-moment of waiting — in line, on the toilet, between tasks — gets filled with scrolling. But boredom serves a critical function: it activates the default mode network, the brain region responsible for creativity, self-reflection, and problem-solving. When you eliminate boredom, you eliminate the mental space where your best ideas and deepest self-awareness emerge.
- **Neuroscience Note:** The default mode network is most active during periods of unfocused rest. It's where your brain consolidates memories, processes emotions, and generates creative insights. Social media suppresses it.
- **Tip:** Next time you feel bored, don't fill it. Just wait. Notice what your mind does when given space.
