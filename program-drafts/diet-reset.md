# Diet Reset — 30 Days

> Status: DRAFT — AWAITING REVIEW
> Last updated: 2026-03-22

## Interview Notes

- **Philosophy:** Blend of discipline, biology, and psychology
- **Restrictions:** Strict clean eating — whole foods only. No processed food, no added sugar, no dairy, no gluten, no alcohol.
- **Recommended mode:** Cold turkey
- **Daily format:** Consistent core challenge, educational content rotates evenly across science (biology), psychology, and practical/discipline topics
- **Success criteria:** Percentage-based — "All meals and snacks were clean whole foods today."
- **Difficulty curve (Cold Turkey):** 4 → 3 → 2 → 2 over weeks 1-4
- **Gradual Build arc:** Hybrid — remove obvious junk first, layer in meal-by-meal discipline, arrive at full strict clean eating
- **Educational mix:** Alternates between "why" (science) and "how" (practical) throughout

## Cold Turkey Mode

### Day 1
- **Challenge:** Eat only whole, unprocessed foods
- **Description:** Day 1 of your reset. Every meal and snack today must come from whole, unprocessed ingredients — vegetables, fruits, lean meats, fish, eggs, nuts, seeds, legumes. No processed food, no added sugar, no dairy, no gluten, no alcohol. If it has a long ingredient list, skip it.
- **Success Criteria:** All meals and snacks were clean whole foods today.
- **Difficulty:** 4
- **Category:** Physical
- **Educational Title:** What "Processed" Actually Means
- **Educational Content:** A processed food is anything that's been significantly altered from its natural state. This includes obvious junk (chips, candy) but also things marketed as healthy — granola bars, flavored yogurt, most breads, deli meat. The rule is simple: if it was made in a factory, it's out. If it grew from the ground or had a mother, it's in.
- **Tip:** Do a kitchen audit. Move anything that doesn't qualify to a box or separate shelf. Out of sight, less temptation.

### Day 2
- **Challenge:** Eat only whole, unprocessed foods
- **Description:** Day 2. Your body is adjusting. You may feel hungrier than usual — that's your brain expecting the easy sugar hits it's used to. Eat enough whole foods to stay full. Don't restrict calories; restrict ingredients.
- **Success Criteria:** All meals and snacks were clean whole foods today.
- **Difficulty:** 4
- **Category:** Physical
- **Educational Title:** The Sugar Withdrawal Is Real
- **Educational Content:** Added sugar triggers the same dopamine pathways as addictive substances. When you cut it abruptly, your brain protests — headaches, irritability, fatigue, and intense cravings are common in the first 3-5 days. This isn't weakness. It's your neurochemistry recalibrating. The discomfort is evidence of how dependent your system had become.
- **Neuroscience Note:** Studies show sugar activates the nucleus accumbens (the brain's reward center) with similar intensity to certain drugs. Withdrawal symptoms peak around days 2-3.
- **Tip:** Eat fruit when sugar cravings hit. The natural fructose takes the edge off while fiber slows absorption.

### Day 3
- **Challenge:** Eat only whole, unprocessed foods
- **Description:** Day 3 — often the hardest day. Cravings may peak today. Remember: this discomfort is temporary and it's proof that change is happening. Push through.
- **Success Criteria:** All meals and snacks were clean whole foods today.
- **Difficulty:** 4
- **Category:** Physical
- **Educational Title:** Why Cravings Lie to You
- **Educational Content:** Cravings feel like hunger, but they're not. True hunger builds gradually and can be satisfied by any food. Cravings are sudden, specific ("I need chocolate"), and driven by habit loops — not nutritional need. When a craving hits, ask: "Am I hungry, or does my brain just want a hit?" If a plain apple doesn't sound appealing, it's a craving, not hunger.
- **Tip:** Drink a full glass of water and wait 15 minutes. Dehydration often masquerades as cravings.

- **Educational Title:** Your Gut Microbiome Is Already Shifting
- **Educational Content:** Your gut hosts trillions of bacteria that influence cravings, mood, and immune function. Different bacteria thrive on different foods — sugar-loving bacteria literally send signals to your brain demanding more sugar. Within 48-72 hours of changing your diet, the bacterial population starts shifting. The sugar-craving bacteria weaken; fiber-loving bacteria grow. Your gut is remodeling itself in real time.
- **Tip:** Eat a variety of vegetables — diversity in your diet feeds diversity in your microbiome.

- **Difficulty:** 4
- **Category:** Physical
- **Educational Title:** How to Read a Food Label (And Why Most "Health Food" Fails)
- **Educational Content:** If you must buy something packaged, the ingredient list tells the truth. Ingredients are listed by weight — if sugar (or its 60+ aliases like dextrose, maltose, "evaporated cane juice") appears in the first 5 ingredients, it's a sugar product. If the list has more than 5-6 recognizable ingredients, it's heavily processed. Better yet: buy foods that don't need labels — produce, meat, fish, eggs.
- **Tip:** Learn sugar's hidden names: high fructose corn syrup, agave nectar, barley malt, rice syrup, dextrose, maltodextrin, sucralose. They're all sugar.

## Gradual Build Mode

### Day 1
- **Challenge:** Cut all sugary drinks
- **Description:** Day 1 starts easy but meaningful. No soda, juice, energy drinks, sweetened coffee, or any drink with added sugar. Water, black coffee, and herbal tea only. This single change eliminates a major source of hidden sugar.
- **Success Criteria:** All beverages today were unsweetened — water, black coffee, or herbal tea only.
- **Difficulty:** 2
- **Category:** Physical
- **Educational Title:** Liquid Sugar — The Invisible Saboteur
- **Educational Content:** A single can of soda contains 39g of sugar — almost your entire recommended daily limit in one drink. Liquid sugar is especially harmful because it bypasses the chewing and satiety signals that slow down solid food consumption. Your body absorbs it almost instantly, causing a blood sugar spike followed by a crash. Cutting sugary drinks is the single highest-impact dietary change you can make.
- **Tip:** Keep a water bottle visible at all times. When you reach for a drink, make water the default.

### Day 2
- **Challenge:** Cut all sugary drinks
- **Description:** Day 2 — same focus on drinks. If you relied on sweetened coffee or energy drinks, today tests that habit. Black coffee is an acquired taste, but you'll get there.
- **Success Criteria:** All beverages today were unsweetened.
- **Difficulty:** 2
- **Category:** Physical
- **Educational Title:** How Your Brain Processes Liquid vs. Solid Calories
- **Educational Content:** Your brain registers liquid and solid calories completely differently. Solid food triggers stretch receptors in the stomach and requires chewing time, both of which signal fullness. Liquid calories skip these mechanisms entirely. Studies show people who drink sugary beverages don't compensate by eating less — they simply consume more total calories without realizing it.
- **Tip:** If black coffee is too bitter, try it with a splash of unsweetened almond milk or cinnamon. Give your palate 3-4 days to adjust.

### Day 3
- **Challenge:** Cut sugary drinks and sugary snacks
- **Description:** Building on drinks, now add sugary snacks to the cut list — candy, cookies, pastries, ice cream, chocolate bars, sweetened granola bars. Replace with whole food snacks: nuts, fruit, vegetables with hummus.
- **Success Criteria:** No sugary drinks or sugary snacks today. All snacks were whole foods.
- **Difficulty:** 3
- **Category:** Physical
- **Educational Title:** The Snacking Trap
- **Educational Content:** Modern snacking barely existed before the 1970s. Food companies created the "snacking occasion" as a marketing strategy to sell more products. Today, the average person eats 2-3 snacks per day, mostly processed. Snacking isn't inherently bad, but what you snack on matters enormously. A handful of almonds and an apple is fuel. A granola bar is a candy bar in disguise.
- **Tip:** Pre-portion snacks in advance: small bags of nuts, cut vegetables, hard-boiled eggs. Preparation defeats temptation.

### Day 4

- **Description:** Day 4 — sugary drinks and snacks still off limits. By now, sugar cravings may be kicking in. This is your brain's dopamine system protesting the loss of easy rewards. Ride it out.
- **Success Criteria:** No sugary drinks or sugary snacks today. All snacks were whole foods.

- **Educational Title:** Dopamine and Your Food Reward System
- **Educational Content:** Sugar activates the same dopamine reward pathway that responds to social media, gambling, and addictive substances.
